System Types

Curtain Wall Technologies

Understanding the different curtain wall system types we fabricate and install.

Stick System

Traditional

Components assembled piece-by-piece on site. Mullions and transoms installed first, then glazing panels added.

Advantages:

  • Lower initial cost
  • Flexible for complex geometries
  • Easier to transport
  • Good for low to mid-rise buildings

Applications:

  • Commercial buildings
  • Shopping centers
  • Office complexes

Unitized System

Advanced

Factory-assembled panels with glass already installed. Complete units lifted and connected on site.

Advantages:

  • Faster installation
  • Better quality control
  • Less on-site labor
  • Ideal for high-rise buildings

Applications:

  • High-rise towers
  • Premium developments
  • Time-critical projects
Glazing

Glass Specifications

Types of glass commonly used in our installations.

Tempered Glass

Heat-treated for safety. Breaks into small, less dangerous pieces.

Laminated Glass

Multiple layers bonded with interlayer. Remains intact when broken.

Insulated Glass (DGU)

Double or triple glazing for thermal and acoustic insulation.

Low-E Glass

Coating that minimizes UV and infrared light without reducing visible light.

Tinted Glass

Color-tinted for solar control and aesthetic purposes.

Reflective Glass

Metallic coating reflects sunlight for solar control.

Frosted Glass

Etched or sandblasted for privacy while allowing light transmission.

Fire-Rated Glass

Certified for fire resistance in specific applications.
