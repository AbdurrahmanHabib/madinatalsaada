System Types

Curtain Wall Technologies

Understanding the different curtain wall system types we fabricate and install.

Stick System

Traditional

Components assembled piece-by-piece on site. Mullions and transoms are installed first, then glazing panels are added individually.

Advantages

  • Lower initial cost
  • Flexible for complex geometries
  • Easier to transport
  • Good for low to mid-rise buildings

Applications

  • Commercial buildings
  • Shopping centres
  • Office complexes

Unitized System

Advanced

Factory-assembled panels with glass already installed. Complete units are lifted and connected on site for rapid enclosure.

Advantages

  • Faster installation speed
  • Better quality control
  • Less on-site labour required
  • Ideal for high-rise buildings

Applications

  • High-rise towers
  • Premium developments
  • Time-critical projects
Glazing

Glass Specifications

Types of glass commonly specified and installed across our projects.

Tempered Glass

Heat-treated for enhanced strength and safety. Breaks into small, less dangerous granular pieces on impact.

Laminated Glass

Multiple layers bonded with PVB or SGP interlayer. Remains intact when broken, providing fall-out protection.

Insulated Glass (DGU)

Double or triple glazed units with sealed air gap for superior thermal and acoustic insulation performance.

Low-E Glass

Microscopically thin coating that minimizes ultraviolet and infrared light without reducing visible light transmission.

Tinted Glass

Colour-tinted during manufacturing for solar heat control and aesthetic consistency across large facades.

Reflective Glass

Metallic oxide coating reflects solar radiation for effective heat rejection in hot-climate applications.

Frosted Glass

Acid-etched or sandblasted surface for privacy screening while maintaining high light transmission levels.

Fire-Rated Glass

Certified for fire resistance up to 120 minutes. Required for atriums, stairwells, and compartment boundaries.
